Fundamentals of GIS Projects

GIS Journey:

I am a first-year PhD student in Geography and Urban Studies. This spring semester, I am enrolled in Fundamentals of Geographic Information Systems with the hopes of learning a new skill to apply in my future research. My research interests lie in anti-displacement strategies in gentrifying neighborhoods and communities. I am extremely interested in working with the Puerto Rican population either in Philadelphia or the archipelago, acknowledging that there have been decades of systemic factors leading to displacement in both geographies. While displacements happen differently, I think there is much to learn from either geography; the story I want to focus on is the Puerto Rican people's resilience and movements for resistance to these changes.



GIS Portfolio

Spring 2024

Map One: Philadelphia Schools & Bike Network

By utilizing three datasets, which included Philly neighborhoods, Philly bike routes, and school types, I could exhibit what charter schools in Philadelphia had enrollments of over 1200 students and which neighborhoods they were located in. After importing the data into ArcGis, I was then able to utilize the Attributes function (Pressing Ctrl + T or the Select by using function in the ribbon). This allowed me to see what data I had to filter through, which included information like class types such as high schools or elementary, demographics, neighborhood locations of the schools, and more. By selecting the specific attributes I needed, in this case charter schools with enrollments over 1200, I could then pinpoint exactly where those schools were. Furthermore, I could then export data features to highlight those specific attributes against the larger map and dataset (by neighborhoods and the charter school demographics). Later after the data was all included, I could then use the symbology function (double-clicking the boxes and icons to change the aesthetics); I adjusted it so it was visually obvious what schools I wanted to highlight.

For this particular map, I wanted to answer the follow question: Where are the large Philadelphia schools that are well connected to the existing bike network?
